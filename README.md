The goal of this Power BI dashboard project is to analyze retail sales performance, profitability, customer behavior, discount effectiveness, product category performance, and regional trends to support strategic business decisions.

---

## Steps Followed

Step 1: Data was imported into Power BI from the Retail Supply Chain & Sales dataset.

Step 2: Data cleaning and transformation were performed using Power Query Editor.

Step 3: Data validation was conducted to ensure consistency across sales, profit, discount, quantity, customer, and order fields.

Step 4: Data modeling was completed to establish relationships between categories, customers, regions, states, and discount groups.

Step 5: DAX measures were created to calculate Revenue, Profit, Orders, Quantity Sold, Lost Revenue, and Average Discount.

Step 6: KPI cards were designed to display:

* Customers
* Net Profit
* Revenue
* Products
* Lost Revenue
* Quantity Sold
* Transactions
* Average Discount

Step 7: Donut charts were created to analyze:

* Profit by Category
* Sales by Category
* Profit by Region
* Profit by Segment
* Order Count by Category
* Order Count by Discount Group

Step 8: Bar and column charts were developed to show:

* Sales and Profit by Category
* Profit by Retail Salesperson
* Profit by Customer Name
* Profit by Discount Level

Step 9: Line and combination charts were created to analyze:

* Sales and Profit by Year
* Profit and Average Discount by State

Step 10: Insight cards were added to highlight top and bottom performers across categories and discount segments.

Step 11: Interactive slicers were incorporated to enable dynamic filtering and detailed analysis.

## [2] Category Performance Analysis

### Total Sales by Category

* Technology generated the highest sales of approximately 836.15K.
* Furniture generated sales of approximately 742K.
* Office Supplies generated sales of approximately 719.05K.

Thus, Technology products were the leading revenue contributors.

---

### Total Profit by Category

* Office Supplies generated the highest profit of approximately 122.49K.
* Technology generated approximately 145.45K profit.
* Furniture generated only 18.45K profit.

Thus, Furniture contributed significantly less profit compared to the other categories despite strong sales performance.

---

### Order Count by Category

* Office Supplies recorded approximately 6.03K orders.
* Technology recorded approximately 2.12K orders.
* Furniture recorded approximately 1.85K orders.

Thus, Office Supplies experienced the highest customer demand based on order volume.

---

### Average Discount by Category

* Furniture received the highest average discount.
* Technology and Office Supplies operated with comparatively lower discount levels.

Thus, Furniture sales rely more heavily on discounting to drive purchases.

## [4] Regional and Segment Analysis

### Profit by Region

* West region generated the highest profit contribution.
* Central region ranked second.
* East and South regions produced comparatively lower profits.

Thus, the West region remains the strongest-performing market.

---

### Profit by Customer Segment

* Consumer segment generated approximately 46.83% of total profit.
* Corporate segment contributed approximately 32.12%.
* Home Office segment accounted for approximately 21.05%.

Thus, Consumer customers remain the most valuable segment.

---

## [5] Discount Analysis

### Profit by Discount Level

* Most profit was generated from products with little or no discount.
* Profit declined significantly as discount percentages increased.
* Some high-discount ranges generated very low or negative profit.

Thus, excessive discounting reduces profitability and should be carefully controlled.

---

### Order Count by Discount Group

* The 0% discount group generated approximately 4.94K orders.
* The 20% discount group generated approximately 3.91K orders.
* Higher discount groups accounted for a much smaller share of orders.

Thus, customers continue purchasing even with minimal discounts, indicating that deep discounts may not be necessary to drive demand.

---

### Discount vs Quantity Relationship

* Most transactions occurred within lower discount ranges.
* Higher discounts did not consistently produce significantly higher sales quantities.

Thus, discount increases do not always translate into proportional sales growth.

---

## [6] State-Level Performance Analysis

### Profit and Average Discount by State

* States such as Indiana and Vermont generated strong profits while maintaining moderate discount levels.
* Some states offered higher discounts but generated lower profits.

Thus, profitability is influenced by factors beyond discounting, including customer demand and product mix.

## Recommendations

1. Increase investment in Technology products, as they generate the highest revenue and strong profitability.
2. Improve Furniture margins by reviewing pricing, sourcing costs, and discount policies.
3. Expand inventory for Office Supplies, which generate the highest order volume and strong profits.
4. Reduce unnecessary high-discount promotions, as profit decreases significantly at higher discount levels.
5. Focus retention efforts on high-value customers who contribute the largest share of profit.
6. Replicate the strategies of top-performing sales representatives across the entire sales team.
7. Expand marketing and distribution activities in the West region and other high-performing markets.
8. Develop state-specific strategies by analyzing regions where discounts are high but profits remain low.
9. Prioritize the Consumer segment through loyalty programs and personalized promotions.
10. Continuously monitor discount effectiveness to maximize revenue while protecting profit margins.
